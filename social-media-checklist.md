# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## DO's

1. Keep your LinkedIn profile updated with your current education, skills, and any projects or certifications you complete — even small ones count in first year.

2. Use your real name on professional platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub. Recruiters search by name and a recognizable profile is easier to find and trust.

3. Think before you post. Ask yourself whether you would be comfortable if your professor, parent, or a future employer saw this. If not, do not post it.

4. Follow people and pages in your field of interest — researchers, companies, developers. What appears in your feed shapes what you learn passively every day.

5. Give credit when you share someone else's work, article, or idea online. Reposting without attribution is a form of plagiarism.

6. Use privacy settings on personal platforms like Instagram and Snapchat. Keep personal content away from professional networks.

7. Engage constructively in comment sections and forums. A thoughtful comment on a technical post is itself a form of networking.

8. Screenshot and save proof of your achievements — completed courses, hackathon participations, coding badges — and add them to your portfolio.

## DON'Ts

1. Do not post anything that could be seen as discriminatory, offensive, or politically inflammatory. Even if deleted later, screenshots last.

2. Do not connect your personal social media handles to your professional profiles unless the content is appropriate for both audiences.

3. Do not share your location, class schedule, or daily routine publicly. It is a safety and privacy risk.

4. Do not engage in arguments or call-out posts on social media. Even if you are right, public conflicts damage your professional image.

5. Do not copy someone else's work and post it as your own — this applies to code, writing, designs, and photographs.

6. Do not accept connection requests from unknown people who immediately ask for personal information, money, or favors. This is a common social engineering tactic.

7. Do not use your professional email address to sign up for social platforms, games, or promotional offers unrelated to your studies.

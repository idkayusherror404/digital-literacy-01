# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist for College Students

## General Online Safety

1. Use a different password for every important account — email, college portal, banking, and social media should never share the same password. Use a password manager like Bitwarden (free) to keep track.

2. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your email and any account linked to money or personal data. Even if someone gets your password, they cannot log in without the second step.

3. Never click links received in SMS, WhatsApp, or email from unknown sources — even if the message looks like it is from a bank, TRAI, or government body. Go directly to the official website instead.

4. Check the URL carefully before entering any login or payment details. Look for HTTPS and make sure the domain is spelled correctly (e.g., sbi.co.in, not sbii.co.in).

5. Do not share OTPs with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, BSNL, or the government. No legitimate organization will ever ask for your OTP over a call or message.

6. Regularly review which apps have permission to access your camera, microphone, contacts, and location. Remove permissions that are not necessary.

## UPI and Financial Safety (Specific to Indian Students)

7. Understand the difference between a UPI payment request and a payment transfer. When you enter your PIN in response to a request notification, you are SENDING money — not receiving it. You do not need to enter your PIN to receive money.

8. Never share your UPI PIN, CVV, or card number over the phone or chat, even if the caller claims to be from your bank's customer care. Call your bank back on the official number if you are unsure.

## Reporting

If you are a victim of any cybercrime or notice suspicious activity:

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 (available 24 hours, 7 days a week)
- Report immediately — delays significantly reduce the chance of recovering lost funds or tracing the attacker.

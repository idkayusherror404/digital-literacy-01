# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Type of Cybercrime
UPI and Online Payment Fraud

## What It Is
UPI fraud refers to the unauthorized transfer of money from a victim's bank account through deception, manipulation, or social engineering using India's Unified Payments Interface (UPI). It is one of the most common cybercrimes targeting college students and young adults in India, largely because UPI is used daily for even small transactions and users often act quickly without verifying requests.

## How It Typically Happens — Step by Step

1. The attacker identifies a target, often someone who has posted a "selling" listing on OLX, Quikr, or a college WhatsApp group.

2. The attacker contacts the seller pretending to be a buyer and shows immediate interest without negotiating the price, which is the first warning sign.

3. The attacker says they will pay via UPI and sends a "payment request" — not a money transfer — to the victim's UPI app. The interface looks similar to a payment confirmation screen.

4. The victim, assuming they are receiving money, enters their UPI PIN to "accept" what is actually a payment request. This sends money out of their account rather than receiving it.

5. In more sophisticated versions, the attacker also sends a fake screenshot of a successful bank transfer and pressures the victim to ship the item immediately.

## Who Is Usually Targeted
Young adults between 18 and 30, students selling second-hand items online, and first-time UPI users who are not yet familiar with the difference between a collect request and a payment.

## Consequences
Immediate financial loss with little chance of recovery if not reported within hours. Victims often feel embarrassed and delay reporting, which reduces the chance of the transaction being traced. In some cases, victims are also blackmailed with their personal details shared during the scam conversation.

## Source
Based on cases documented by the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and reported by the Press Trust of India between 2022 and 2024.
